The strategy of binary search
â€¢We call an element of the sequence a candidate if, at the current 
stage of the search, we cannot rule out that this item matches the 
target
â€¢The algorithm maintains two parameters, lowand high , such that all 
the candidate entries have index at least lowand at most high
â€¢Initially, low = 0 and high = nâˆ’1 . We then compare the target value to 
the median candidate, that is, the item data[mid] with index
mid = (low+high)/2
The strategy of binary search
â€¢If the target equals data[mid] , then we have found the item we 
are looking for, and the search terminates successfully
â€¢If target < data[mid], then we recur on the first half of the 
sequence, that is, on the interval of indices from low to midâˆ’1
â€¢If target > data[mid], then we recur on the second half of the 
sequence, that is, on the interval of indices from mid+1 to high
